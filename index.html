📚 WM340 / WM350 — What to Re-Read First

These prerequisite topics feed directly into WM360:

🔬 Cell Structure & Membrane Transport — WM340 Ch 3–4 Blood cells and gas exchange hinge on membranes, osmosis, and active transport. Review before Week 1.
🧠 Autonomic Nervous System — WM350 Ch 14 ANS controls heart rate, blood pressure, and digestion — essential for Weeks 2–3 and 8–9.
💧 Fluid & Electrolyte Balance — WM350 Ch 26 Blood composition and lymphatic fluid make no sense without plasma vs. interstitial fluid basics.
⚡ Action Potentials & Cardiac Muscle — WM340 Ch 9 Week 2's cardiac conduction system requires knowing how muscle cell action potentials work.
🦴 Connective Tissue Types — WM340 Ch 4 Blood is connective tissue. Knowing the classification makes Week 1 blood composition intuitive.
🔁 Endocrine Hormones — WM350 Ch 16 EPO, ADH, and aldosterone intersect with blood and blood pressure across multiple weeks.

💡 Strategies for a Doctoral A+
🌿 Pre-read every week — The syllabus assigns 3–4 hrs of reading before class. Dr. Sadrolsadot builds on it. Show up prepared.
🧠 Quiz yourself within 24 hrs of class — Use this tool the morning after every Thursday. Active recall beats re-reading 3:1.
☯️ Connect TCM as you go — Dr. Sadrolsadot has a PhD in TCM. Ask Claude how each system maps to the Five Zang organs. This is your competitive edge.
🥋 Qi Gong & Tai Chi crossover — You're already studying how Qi flows. The lymphatic and cardiovascular systems are literally the Western map of that flow. Make the connection in your notes.
📋 One-page sheet per week — Condense each lecture into: key structures · functions · imbalances. These become your midterm/final cram sheets.
⏰ Book virtual office hours before the midterm — Go once before Week 5. Ask what he emphasizes. It's free points.
